Every stock on MomentumRadar carries a Momentum Score from 0 to 100. Unlike many "proprietary" ratings, ours is fully open — this page shows the exact formula, so you can verify any number on the site yourself.

Step 1 — measure five returns

For each stock we compute the percentage price change over five lookbacks, using dividend- and split-adjusted daily closes: 1 day, 1 week (5 trading days), 1 month (21), 1 quarter (63) and 1 year (252).

Step 2 — convert each return to a 0–100 sub-score

Raw percentages aren't comparable across timeframes — a 3% day is huge, a 3% year is nothing. So each return is passed through a squashing function that maps it onto a 0–100 scale:

sub-score = 50 + 50 × tanh(return ÷ scale)

where the scale reflects what a "big" move is for that timeframe: 3% for a day, 6% for a week, 12% for a month, 25% for a quarter, 60% for a year. A return of zero always scores 50 (neutral); strongly positive returns approach 100; strongly negative approach 0. The tanh curve also means extreme outliers can't dominate — a +300% meme-stock year scores barely higher than a +150% year.

Step 3 — weight and combine

TimeframeWeightWhy
1 day10%Mostly noise; small influence only
1 week20%Short-term direction
1 month30%Best early-trend signal — highest weight
1 quarter25%Trend confirmation
1 year15%Big-picture context

The final score is the weighted average of the five sub-scores, rounded to a whole number. If a stock hasn't traded long enough for a timeframe (e.g. a recent IPO with no 1-year history), the remaining weights are re-scaled proportionally.

A worked example

Suppose a stock returned +1.5% (1D), +4% (1W), +10% (1M), +22% (3M), +45% (1Y):

1D: 50+50×tanh(1.5/3)=73 · 1W: 50+50×tanh(4/6)=79 · 1M: 50+50×tanh(10/12)=84 · 3M: 50+50×tanh(22/25)=86 · 1Y: 50+50×tanh(45/60)=82

Weighted: 0.10×73 + 0.20×79 + 0.30×84 + 0.25×86 + 0.15×82 = 82 → "Strong".

Reading the bands

80–100 Strong — rising consistently across most timeframes. 65–79 Good — solid but not uniform strength. 50–64 Moderate — mixed picture. 35–49 Weak — more down than up. 0–34 Very weak — falling across most horizons.

The 90-day history — the part most sites don't show

A single score is a snapshot. On every stock card we also recalculate the score for each of the last 90 trading days and plot it, so you can see the trajectory: a stock at 85 and climbing is a different situation from a stock at 85 and sliding from 95. Direction of the score often matters more than the level.

What the score is not

It is not a buy signal, a price target or a prediction. It contains no information about valuation, debt, profits or management — which is why every stock card links directly to the company's financial statements. A perfect 100 tells you one thing only: the price has been rising unusually consistently. Whether that continues is exactly the risk you take.
