ℹ️ About MomentumRadar

MomentumRadar exists to solve a real problem: knowing which stocks are gaining momentum across global markets is hard for ordinary investors. Professional traders have expensive terminals; everyone else gets fragmented apps that cover one country at a time. We built a free, beginner-friendly window into global stock momentum — 13 markets, one transparent score, plain English.

The site is 100% free: no account, no subscription, no paywall. It is supported by advertising clearly labelled as such.

Where the data comes from

All prices are real market data from Yahoo Finance's public API (with Stooq as a fallback source), using dividend- and split-adjusted daily closing prices. The entire site is rebuilt automatically every trading day after the US close; the exact refresh timestamp appears in the header of every page. If a market's data source is temporarily unreachable, we display the most recent good data clearly labelled with its date — we never silently show stale numbers as current.

Quotes are end-of-day, not live. Returns are computed in each market's local currency.

The Momentum Score — full open methodology

Unlike "proprietary black-box" ratings, our score is fully documented and reproducible:

  1. Measure five returns per stock from adjusted closes: 1 day, 1 week (5 trading days), 1 month (21), 1 quarter (63), 1 year (252).
  2. Normalise each to 0–100 via 50 + 50 × tanh(return ÷ scale), with scales of 3% (1D), 6% (1W), 12% (1M), 25% (3M), 60% (1Y). Zero return always maps to a neutral 50; the tanh curve prevents extreme outliers dominating.
  3. Combine with fixed weights: 10% daily, 20% weekly, 30% monthly, 25% quarterly, 15% annual. Missing timeframes (e.g. recent IPOs) redistribute their weight proportionally.

The result is a whole number from 0 to 100: 80–100 Strong · 65–79 Good · 50–64 Moderate · 35–49 Weak · 0–34 Very Weak. We also recalculate the score for each of the past 90 trading days to plot every stock's momentum trajectory. A worked example lives in the score explainer.

What we rank

In each market we score a curated universe of the most liquid large-cap stocks (listed openly in the site's configuration) and display the top 10 by score. We deliberately exclude illiquid small caps, where price moves are easy to manipulate and hard for ordinary investors to trade fairly.

The top-pick analysis is generated from data — on purpose

Every "why it ranks #1" paragraph is generated automatically from that day's actual numbers: returns across timeframes, distance from the 52-week high, volatility, and the direction of the momentum score itself. That means the analysis can never be stale or based on someone's opinion — and it's why every stock card links to the company's real financial statements, so you can go from our momentum snapshot to genuine fundamental research in one click.
